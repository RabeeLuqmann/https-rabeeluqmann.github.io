<h2>About the Author</h2>

<p>Rabe Luqmann is a German-Jordanian author with a passion for exploring the complexities of love, desire, and the human condition across cultures. His debut novel, <em>Bound by Destiny</em>, captivated readers with its poignant portrayal of arranged marriage in India, blending tradition with modernity.</p>

<p>With <em>Endorphin: The Ache of Wanting</em>, Luqmann plunged into the visceral depths of longing, addiction, and obsessive emotion, crafting a raw, lyrical narrative that lingers in the mind. <em>The Daughter Who Was Not Named</em> solidifies his place as a bold voice, exploring the dark heart.</p>

<p>Luqmanns writing is marked by vivid cultural tapestries and powerful storytelling, whether unraveling the tensions between duty and passion or probing the addictive nature of unfulfilled desire. His work resonates with universal truths while celebrating the nuances of diverse worlds.</p>

<p>When not writing, Luqmann immerses himself in philosophy, psychology, and the artsfueling his fascination with the forces that drive human connection.</p>
